Nelson Mandela

1918-2013

Description

Nelson Mandela, born on July 18, 1918, in the village of Mvezo, Eastern Cape, was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary and political leader. He dedicated his life to dismantling the oppressive apartheid system. Mandela served 27 years in prison for his activism before his release in 1990...
In 1994, Mandela became South Africa's first black president, leading the nation through a process of reconciliation. His presidency marked a turning point, fostering unity and ending institutionalized racial discrimination. Mandela passed away on December 5, 2013, leaving a lasting legacy of resilience, forgiveness, and the pursuit of justice. His contributions to human rights earned him global admiration, making him an iconic figure in the fight against oppression.
